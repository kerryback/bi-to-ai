# Acme Corp Employee Handbook

## Chapter 1: Employment Policies

### 1.1 At-Will Employment

Employment at Acme Corp is on an at-will basis. Either the employee or the company may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause, and with or without notice. Nothing in this handbook creates a contract of employment or guarantees employment for any specific duration.

### 1.2 Equal Employment Opportunity

Acme Corp is committed to equal employment opportunity. We do not discriminate based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, veteran status, sexual orientation, gender identity, or any other protected characteristic. This policy applies to all aspects of employment including hiring, promotion, compensation, benefits, and termination.

### 1.3 Work Hours and Schedules

Standard business hours are Monday through Friday, 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Core hours during which all employees are expected to be available are 10:00 AM to 3:00 PM. Employees may arrange flexible schedules with their manager's approval, provided core hours are observed. Remote work is permitted up to three days per week with manager approval.

## Chapter 2: Compensation and Benefits

### 2.1 Pay Periods

Employees are paid on a bi-weekly basis, with pay periods ending every other Friday. Direct deposit is required for all employees. Pay stubs are available through the HR portal beginning on the Thursday before each payday.

### 2.2 Overtime Policy

Non-exempt employees are eligible for overtime pay at 1.5 times their regular hourly rate for all hours worked in excess of 40 hours per workweek. Overtime must be pre-approved by the employee's direct supervisor. Unauthorized overtime may result in disciplinary action, though all hours worked will be compensated.

### 2.3 Bonus Program

Annual performance bonuses are discretionary and based on individual performance, department performance, and company financial results. Bonus eligibility requires a minimum of six months of continuous employment before the end of the fiscal year. Target bonus percentages vary by level: individual contributors (5-10%), managers (10-15%), directors (15-25%), and vice presidents (20-35%).

### 2.4 Benefits Enrollment

New employees may enroll in benefits within 30 days of their start date. The annual open enrollment period is November 1-15. Benefits include medical insurance (three plan options), dental insurance, vision insurance, life insurance (1x salary provided, supplemental available), and a 401(k) retirement plan with a 4% company match.

## Chapter 3: Time Off and Leave

### 3.1 Paid Time Off (PTO)

PTO accrues based on years of service. Employees with 0-2 years receive 15 days per year. Employees with 3-5 years receive 20 days. Employees with 6-10 years receive 25 days. Employees with more than 10 years receive 30 days. PTO may be carried over up to a maximum of 10 days into the following year. Any accrued PTO beyond 10 days is forfeited on January 1 unless state law provides otherwise.

### 3.2 Sick Leave

Employees receive 10 days of paid sick leave per year, accrued at approximately 0.83 days per month. Sick leave may be used for the employee's own illness or to care for an immediate family member. A doctor's note is required for absences of three or more consecutive days. Unused sick leave does not carry over and is not paid out upon separation.

### 3.3 Holidays

Acme Corp observes the following paid holidays: New Year's Day, Martin Luther King Jr. Day, Presidents' Day, Memorial Day, Juneteenth, Independence Day, Labor Day, Thanksgiving Day, the day after Thanksgiving, Christmas Eve, Christmas Day, and New Year's Eve. When a holiday falls on a weekend, the nearest weekday is observed.

### 3.4 Parental Leave

Acme Corp provides 12 weeks of paid parental leave for the birth, adoption, or foster placement of a child. This leave is available to all eligible employees regardless of gender. Employees must have completed at least one year of service to be eligible. Parental leave may be taken in a continuous block or, with manager approval, intermittently within six months of the qualifying event.

## Chapter 4: Travel and Expense Policy

### 4.1 Travel Authorization

All business travel must be pre-approved by the employee's manager. Travel exceeding $5,000 in total estimated cost requires additional approval from the department head. International travel requires approval from a vice president. Travel should be booked at least 14 days in advance when possible to secure the best rates.

### 4.2 Expense Reimbursement

Expense reports must be submitted within 30 days of incurring the expense. Receipts are required for all expenses over $25. Meals are reimbursed up to $75 per day for domestic travel and $100 per day for international travel. Alcohol is not reimbursable. Mileage for personal vehicle use is reimbursed at the current IRS rate.

### 4.3 Corporate Credit Cards

Corporate credit cards are available for employees who travel frequently. Monthly statements must be reconciled and submitted with receipts within 10 business days of the statement date. Personal charges on corporate credit cards are strictly prohibited and may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination. Lost or stolen cards must be reported immediately to the finance department.

## Chapter 5: Information Security

### 5.1 Acceptable Use

Company technology resources are provided for business purposes. Limited personal use is permitted provided it does not interfere with work performance, consume excessive resources, or violate any company policy. Employees must not install unauthorized software on company devices without IT approval.

### 5.2 Data Classification

Company data is classified into four categories. Public data may be freely shared. Internal data is for employee use only. Confidential data requires specific authorization to access and must be encrypted in transit and at rest. Restricted data (including customer PII, financial records, and trade secrets) requires the highest level of protection and may only be accessed by authorized personnel with a documented business need.

### 5.3 AI Usage Policy

Employees may use AI tools (including Claude, ChatGPT, and similar services) for business purposes subject to the following restrictions. Public or internal data may be used with approved AI tools on enterprise plans. Confidential data may only be used with AI tools that have been specifically approved by the Information Security team and that provide contractual guarantees against training on company data. Restricted data must never be submitted to external AI services. All AI-generated content used in external communications or formal business decisions must be reviewed by a human before use.

### 5.4 Password and Authentication

All employees must use passwords of at least 12 characters that include uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters. Multi-factor authentication is required for all company systems. Passwords must be changed every 90 days. Employees must not share passwords or authentication credentials with anyone, including IT support staff. IT will never ask for your password.

## Chapter 6: Return and Refund Policy

### 6.1 Customer Returns

Customers may return products within 30 days of purchase for a full refund, provided the product is in its original packaging and unused condition. Products returned between 31 and 60 days are eligible for store credit only. No returns are accepted after 60 days. Digital products and customized items are non-refundable.

### 6.2 Enterprise Customer Returns

Enterprise customers operating under a Master Service Agreement (MSA) follow the return terms specified in their contract. In the absence of specific return terms in the MSA, enterprise customers are eligible for returns within 45 days of delivery. Enterprise returns exceeding $10,000 in value require approval from the VP of Sales.

### 6.3 Defective Products

Products found to be defective may be returned at any time within the warranty period (one year from date of purchase for hardware, 90 days for software). Acme Corp will, at its discretion, repair the product, replace it with an equivalent product, or issue a full refund. Shipping costs for defective product returns are covered by Acme Corp.
